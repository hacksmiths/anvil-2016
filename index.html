About Us

The Event

Anvil Hack II is a student run hackathon focused on the creative applications of technology (this is not a prototyping session for your new startup!) taking place at a centre of creative computing research - Goldsmiths University of London

Use your skills to make something wonderful, arty, musical, or just plain old awesome. We'll provide you with food, drink, electricity and WiFi, so that you can get on with the real work - making cool things!

The Venue

The event will be held at the amazing St James Hatcham, usually used as an exhibition area.



The University

Goldsmiths is a world class institute for creative technology. This makes it the perfect place to host Anvil Hack. Goldsmiths is looking forward to welcoming you to our wonderful campus on the weekend of the 7th May.



The Society

We aim to provide opportunities for a diverse and inclusive group of Goldsmiths Hackers (henceforth known as 'Hacksmiths') to interact, collaborate, share knowledge, and create things with one another, and a creative environment conducive to such activities.

An audio-visual hacking experience

  • Oljud

    Terry Clark and Gustaf Svenungsson perform their out-of body movement controlled musical experience piece using a Kinect, Processing and Ableton Live, all connected via OSC.

  • AD/DA

    Vytautas Niedvaras and William Primett perform their piece exploring tension between physical and digital realms. Using Teensy micro-controllers, flex sensors, Max-MSP and a plethra of other Custom hardware and software.

FAQ

Should I come to this hackathon?

Yes*. Next question!

But

No buts! Come to the hackathon!

I've never been to a hackathon before!

Now is the best time to start

Do you own my hack?

What? No, of course not, we're not lawyers. We don't even have lawyers!

How much?

How much? No. It's not. No much. How more not much could it get? None. None more no much. (for clarity over comedy here, it's free. no excuse not to come)

Can I work on projects I start before the hack/coursework/working for the man/working for a living/working nine to five?

Dolly Parton, you're welcome. No prize eligibility though, in the interest of fairness

Can I turn up and be an asshole?

WHO EVEN ASKS THAT! NO! OF COURSE NOT! We adhere to the MLH code of conduct, and we have big angry men to throw you out if you do something awful.

How big should teams be?

Four. No more, no less. Or less, if you want. No more though, that'd open the door to all kinds of crazy hacking of the hacking system

*Please note that due to legal constraints, we're not able to organise for under 18s to attend. We're sorry! Write to your MP.
